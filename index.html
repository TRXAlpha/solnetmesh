Ghost

Ghost is a distributed autonomous system designed for privacy and local control. Intelligence is placed where it is most effective—at the edge—rather than being centralized by default.

The system runs entirely on owned hardware, utilizing custom memory formats redesigned from scratch to optimize for local-first constraints.

Biopotential Sensing

An experimental interface designed to read raw biological signals without institutional medical equipment.

The initial analog prototypes revealed the extreme difficulty of signal isolation in non-lab environments. High susceptibility to electromagnetic noise rendered early data unusable, necessitating a complete pivot toward isolated digital signal processing (DSP) pipelines.

Embedded Prototypes

A series of hardware studies testing the physical limits of autonomy. These prototypes focus on the energy trade-offs required for intelligence to exist without a grid connection.

Multiple iterations failed specifically due to power constraints, establishing the "hard limits" for the current Ghost hardware specifications.
